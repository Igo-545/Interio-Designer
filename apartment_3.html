Small studio

apartment

01

Guest room

The interior was designed for a young man. In order to maximize the space and fill it with more light and air, the old one-room apartment was converted into an open-plan studio, where full-size bed was located and separated from the living room by a glass partition.

02

Materials

Laconic and minimalistic interior items were selected for this interior. Dining table Olberg in black MDF and enamel, Sgib metal black coffee table with bizarre geometry, Wire Frame Dining Chair by West Elm which was inspired by mid-century school chairs, but with head-of-the-class comfort and style with a cushioned seat and back plus an angular, exposed metal frame for modern clarity. The light style of RIGA LUX CORNER sofa in pastel tones by Mexo, as if floating on barely noticeable metal supports.

03

Kitchen

The interior turned out to be minimalistic, bright and at the same time cozy thanks to using of wood and textiles. To add mood to the monochrome interior and make it more “alive,” deep blue blocks were added to the kitchen facades, hallway and rack shelving.
